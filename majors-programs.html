Majors

  • Accounting
    • Business Admin B.S.

      Business Administration majors can select a concentration in accounting. They'll develop in-depth understanding of accounting and report preparation, as well as the ethical and business implications of financial reporting. They'll also explore tax law and other regulations affecting organizations from small businesses up to international nonprofits.

  • Animation
    • Cinematic Arts and Technology B.A.

      The Cinematic Arts major offers courses that develop students' aesthetic and technical skills to manipulate live-action video and produce 2D animation. They'll also learn the principles of storytelling, film history, how to find funding for their projects, and how to distribute their work.

    • Communication Design B.S.

      The Cinematic Arts major offers courses that develop students' aesthetic and technical skills to manipulate live-action video and produce 2D animation. They'll also learn the principles of storytelling, film history, how to find funding for their projects, and how to distribute their work.

  • Agribusiness
    • Business Admin B.S.

      Business Administration majors can select a concentration in agribusiness. In addition to overall business skills in marketing and human resource management, they'll develop industry-specific expertise in areas such as the agricultural supply chain, environmental regulations and plant and soil science.

  • History
    • Human Communication B.A.

      HCOM majors can select a concentration in History, Oral History and New Media. This major takes a storytelling approach to history. Students work with archives, memoirs, autobiographies, photographs, oral histories, and other narrative forms to find stories from the past and give them voice in the present.

    • Social & Behavioral Sciences B.A.

      The SBS major has two history-focused tracks: one that prepares students to become high school history or social studies teachers, and one that focuses on social history -- the study of groups and social movements and how they have shaped society.

    • Global Studies B.A.

      The Global Studies major explores U.S. foreign policy and world history. GS majors analyze global social, economic and political problems and seek solutions for issues such as humanitarian crises, environmental sustainability, the political economy, religious conflict and women's rights.
